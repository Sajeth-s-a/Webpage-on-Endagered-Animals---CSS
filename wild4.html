Cetaceans Fighting For
Survival

The threats facing these gorgeous marine mammals have shifted through the decades; from demand
for whale products driving exploitative over-hunting to threats from bycatch in poorly regulated
commercial fishing, to pollution and climate change.
Often multiple threats acting simultaneously are shrinking populations and disrupt their underwater
range, feeding and mating patterns.
It’s hard to imagine our seas without the gentle calls of the blue whale or the perky chatter of a pod
of dolphins leaping through the waves, but that’s a serious possibility if we do not work to protect these complex and mysterious
creatures. Beyond their historic role in the marine environment, whales, dolphins and porpoises play an increasingly vital role in photographic tourism, and their presence alerts us to pollution or fish decline. We need strong
legislation that will protect these incredible,-blooded mammals and keep our oceans stable. We need to
fight to save our whales, dolphins and porpoises for future generations.
